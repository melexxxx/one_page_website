    Roller Skating!

    Roller skating is the act of traveling on surfaces with roller skates. It is a recreational activity, a sport, and a form of transportation. Roller rinks and skate parks are built for roller skating, though it also takes place on streets, sidewalks, and bike paths.

    Types of skates

    There are many types of Roller Skates, with various sub-categories. Depending on what your preference is you can choose from recreational or aggressive. You can go for 3, 4, or even 5 wheels base. There is something for everyone.

    Quad-skates. Perfect for beginners. Easier to balance left and right. Harder to balance front to back. These will not give you much speed but are perfect for smooth surfaces. Tons of fun!
    Three-wheels base inline skates. Tricky to use as your center of gravity is higher and the ground-wheels surface is much smaller. When mastered, these skates will compensate you with speed and freedom of movement on most surfaces.
    Four-wheels base inline skates. More stable than 3 wheels. You sacrifice speed over control. Most popular, often chosen by newbies. Perfect for city exploring and daily exercising.
    Five-wheels base inline skates. Even more stability than 4 wheels. Fairly new and less common than other types. These are 3 and four wheels combined. Less control and turnability for better speed and grip on corners.
    Serious speed. Performance leaning skates. Used to attack long distances. Hardest to use.
    Skate park focused. Not usable on most surfaces. Flat wheels provide more balance, sacrificing speed. A lot of fun when used on perfectly smooth surfaces.
